EXPERT ADVICE

When Should You Replace a Consumer Unit?

Your consumer unit is the heart of your home's electrical installation. While many older units continue to function, modern consumer units provide significantly improved protection against electric shock, overloads and electrical faults. Replacing an outdated unit can improve safety, make future electrical work easier and help ensure your installation meets current standards.

⚠️ Signs It May Be Time for an Upgrade

  • Frequent tripping of circuits.
  • Old rewireable fuse board.
  • Plastic consumer unit.
  • No RCD protection.
  • Burn marks or overheating.
  • Adding an EV charger or extension.

✅ Benefits of a Modern Consumer Unit

  • Improved electrical safety.
  • RCBO protection for individual circuits.
  • Optional surge protection.
  • Safer fault isolation.
  • Compliance with current UK standards.
  • Suitable for future electrical upgrades.

COST GUIDE

What Affects the Cost of Replacing a Consumer Unit?

Every installation is different. While our calculator provides a realistic estimate, the final cost depends on the condition of your existing installation and the work required to bring it up to current standards.

Number of Circuits

Homes with more electrical circuits generally require larger consumer units and additional testing.

Condition of Existing Wiring

Older properties may require remedial work before a new consumer unit can be safely installed.

Main Earthing & Bonding

Your electrician will inspect the earthing and bonding arrangements to ensure they meet current requirements.

Surge Protection

Many homeowners now choose to include surge protection devices to help protect modern appliances and electronics.

Additional Circuits

If you're planning an EV charger, garden office or extension, choosing a larger consumer unit now can save money later.

Accessibility

The location of the existing consumer unit can affect installation time and complexity.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Consumer Unit FAQs

How much does it cost to replace a consumer unit?

Most modern consumer unit replacements fall within a typical price range, although the final cost depends on the number of circuits, the condition of the existing installation and whether any additional work is required.

How long does a replacement take?

Most straightforward replacements are completed within one working day, including testing and certification.

Will I receive a certificate?

Yes. A compliant installation should include the appropriate electrical certification together with Building Regulations notification where required.

Can I replace my own consumer unit?

Replacing a consumer unit is not considered a DIY job. It involves testing, certification and compliance with Building Regulations, and should only be carried out by a suitably qualified electrician.

Should I replace a plastic consumer unit?

Many older plastic consumer units continue to operate safely, but there are situations where upgrading to a modern metal consumer unit may be appropriate, particularly if major electrical work is being carried out or additional protection is required.
